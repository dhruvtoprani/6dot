The Problem

Sensory Overload

Many visually impaired users report cognitive fatigue and frustration from constant audio input when using screen readers.

Social Discomfort

Students at the RCPD (MSU) shared that screen readers and assistive earphones feel intrusive during social interactions, leading to embarrassment or avoidance.

Bulky & Inconvenient Devices

Most Braille displays are large, heavy, and impractical for daily use. 6Dot offers a compact alternative that snaps onto the back of your phone and fits in your pocket.

“People stare. They think I’m ignoring them when I wear earphones all the time. They call me names when I use the screen reader. I just want to feel normal...”

— John Doe, MSU Student (RCPD)

Our Solution

A quiet revolution in the palm of your hand — made possible by elegant engineering.

Real-Time Braille

Receive messages, directions, and articles in real-time — translated instantly into tactile Braille characters.

Pocket-Sized & Discreet

Smaller than a power bank. Snaps seamlessly onto your phone, designed for all-day, everyday use.

Screenless, Soundless

Forget loud readers or constant headphones — experience quiet, dignified, private accessibility anywhere.

Arduino Nano Brain

The onboard Arduino Nano handles all character logic and timing, acting as the intelligent heart of the device.

Miniature Solenoid Actuation

Each Braille pin is raised/lowered by compact solenoids, enabling fast, tactile feedback within a compact form.

Bluetooth-Driven Input

Messages are streamed wirelessly via Bluetooth and decoded on the fly — no cords, no screen, just touch.
